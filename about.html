IMP and Me

Starting this course I had good expectations of learning more about front-end web designing, something which interests me (probably above back-end). I have, I think, a pretty good understanding of the technologies we touched upon during the class, but I learnt things that I wasn't aware before.

For example, the chrome developer tools, although I was familiar with the console, I didn't know that I could simulate the view of the webpages on different platforms (pretty handy for testing responsiveness), as well as write javascript in there to tamper with the scripting of the page.


I also learnt about Ajax, something I hadn't done before. Websockets, for example, is really interesting, and seem to simplify applications such as chats or data feeds.


In addition, this course refreshed my memory about Node, that I am familiar with, but hadn't touched for a little while.


In all introspection, I would say that I am quite confident in writing javascript, but less so for CSS. Many times I had to check how to do this and that using CSS. This course helped me get the habit of using the most basic 'rules' of designing a responsive website using CSS.


Regarding the conventions, I was familair with them, and eventhough I think I apply them constantly, I still can improve in a few things : Constant and efficient naming of variables, and indenting (I sometimes oversee this, forgetting that others may need to read my code).


Most of all, good logic and vision of a website's functionnality and design, on top of good, clear writing of code, is what I aim for and what this course helped me improve and train on. Knowing the capabilities and limits of each technology is also my main goal, as I believe it is the most important thing to know!
